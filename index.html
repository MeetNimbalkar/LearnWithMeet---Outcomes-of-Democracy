Outcomes of Democracy

EXERCISE
Q.1
How does democracy produce an accountable, responsive and legitimate government?
Or "Democracies produce an accountable, responsive and legitimate governments." Support the statement with suitable examples.
Or "Democracy is an accountable, responsible and legitimate government." Explain the statement.
CBSE 2024
Ans.
Democracy produces an accountable, responsive and legitimate government in the following ways:
Accountable Government
Democracy produces an accountable government as the people have the right to choose their representatives through the electoral process. These elected representatives form the government and participate in the decision-making process on behalf of the people. This is known as transparency. If these elected representatives do not work in a proper way, people have a chance to not elect them in next election.
Responsive Government
Democracy produces a responsive government. As democratic government is elected by the people, it is people's own government. It promotes the formation of public opinion and take care of the needs and expectation of the people.
Legitimate Government
Further, democratic government is a legitimate government because regular election after a fixed time is its key feature. It may be slow, less efficient but it is the people's own legitimate government.
Q.2
What are the conditions under which democracies accommodate social diversities?
Ans.
Democracy is considered to be the most suitable form of government to accommodate these diversities as it aims at giving equal rights and opportunities to all the citizens irrespective of caste, religion, colour, sex, race, etc.
Q.3
Give arguments to support or oppose the following assertions.
(a) Industrialised countries can afford democracy but the poor need dictatorship to become rich.
(b) Democracy cannot reduce inequality of incomes between different citizens.
(c) Government in poor countries should spend less on poverty reduction, health, education and spend more on industries and infrastructure.
(d) In democracy, all citizens have one vote, which means that there is absence of any domination and conflict.
Ans.
(a) This statement is incorrect. If we compare democracy and dictatorship regimes between 1950 to 2000, we find countries under dictatorship to be more economically advanced. But, if we compare between less developed countries, the difference is negligible. For example, India being a third world nation, now becomes one of the fast growing economies of the world. On the other hand, Zimbabwe which was a fairly prosperous nation, has run into huge international debt with the progression of Robert Mugabe's rule. Thus, it is not always proper to say that the poor need dictatorship to become rich.
(b) This statement is incorrect. Democracy reduces the inequality of incomes between citizens. For example, the Minimum Wages Act, enacted by the Indian Government have helped in increasing the per capita income of the country. Thereby, making its citizens more prosperous and reducing inequality in incomes of citizens.
(c) This statement is not correct as the government in poor countries should maintain a balance between investment on poverty, food, clothing, health, education and in industries and infrastructure. A country needs to spend on all the sectors maintaining a balance, so that every sector helps the other sector to grow.
(d) This statement is not correct. In democracy, every citizen has one vote, political parties cannot avoid any social group. The political parties have to formulate policies which will benefit all the groups. But all these do not mean that conflicts are totally absent in a democracy. They can happen if the demands of one social group are against the demands of the other social group. Democracy only avoids the possibility of such conflicts by giving everyone equal rights and freedom in the society.
Q.4
Elaborate the outcomes of democracy.
Ans.
Democracy produces several positive outcomes which are as follows:
1. Accountable Government
Democracy ensures that government officials are answerable to the people. Regular elections, transparency in governance, and right to information make the government accountable for its actions and decisions.
2. Responsive Government
Democratic governments respond to the needs and expectations of the people. Public opinion, media, and civil society organizations influence government policies and ensure that people's voices are heard.
3. Legitimate Government
Democracy provides legitimacy to the government through free and fair elections. People accept the government's authority because they have chosen it through their votes.
4. Better Decision Making
Democratic governments involve consultation and discussion before making decisions. This leads to better quality decisions as different perspectives are considered.
5. Dignity and Freedom
Democracy enhances the dignity of citizens by providing them with fundamental rights, freedom of expression, and equal treatment before law regardless of their social background.
6. Accommodation of Social Diversity
Democracy provides a mechanism to handle social differences and conflicts. It allows different groups to express their grievances peacefully and work towards solutions through dialogue.
